About Me

About

I'm a furniture major from North Carolina, land of the famed beaches and mountains. Being outdoors hiking and exploring my surroundings makes me feel like I can breathe better and has always influenced my work. Though I began my time at SCAD as an Interior Design major, I quickly fell in love with the physicality of furniture making. Studying the qualities of wood as I watch my idea come to life is ever-gratifying. I love that the trees I grew up playing in have become my livelihood, and though making is a passion of mine, I've become fascinated with the furniture market and trend analysis in the process. If you'd like to discuss the best N.C. beaches, see pictures of my dogs, or have any other business inquiries, you can contact me here!

Furniture

Blushing Surf Coffee Table

This collection is based on the trend of blushed tones and soft curved forms. With the Blushing Curves Colleciton, each piece embodies a sense of playfulness and pop of color.

Frill Ottoman

This ottoman is made from donated fabric from Kravet and donated wood frames and upholstery spring units from Legget & Platt. This design correlates to the Blushing Curves Collection.

Trinity Candelabra

The Trinity Candelabra is made from round metal tubing that was MIG welded togther. This design is simple yet interesting.

Hangmans Light

This is not only a light, but also a coat rack. This piece is made from welded metal and concrete. The shade of the light is casted concrete that is wired through the square metal tubing. The cording is hand weaved parachute cord.

Dimple Lamp

This table lamp is constructed of only a few parts; 3D printed feet, CNC walnut base, laser cut leather, small metal bars, and a lightbulb. The Dimple Lamp is clean and elegant expressing a lot of softness.

Whisk Chair

The Whisk Chair was made around the designs of Emeco. The chair seat is made completely out of recycled cosmetic waste, which is an 80 billion dollar industry that produces 120 billion units of cosmetic waste every year. To complement the recycled seat, it is paired with the classic aluminum look of Emeco.

Shaker Table

This Shaker Table is made from poplar and has no hardware. It has a custom laser engraved drawer that hads some small detail. This piece shows a lot of hand craftsmenship.

Interior

Kavilah

This was a collaboration project between interior and furniture design students. The design intent for this project was to create a beautiful and simple interior that lets the clients enjoy the beautiful view of the property. The design was based around the name of the restaurant, Kavilah, meaning stretch of sand in Greek. While the ocean has a fusion of materials, sand and water, the interior will encompass the same sense of movement and fusion. The mixture between the ocean and the sand provides the perfect example of mixed materials while having a sense of fluidity. The space will provide a relaxing and inviting feeling for clients as they enjoy amazing food and drinks. With the combination of natural materials and soft textures, it combines the feel of a natural outdoor environment with an interior space.
